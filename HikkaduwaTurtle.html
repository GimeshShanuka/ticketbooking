Turtle Hatchery Hikkaduwa..

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is a turtle conservation project located in the town of Hikkaduwa in Sri Lanka. The hatchery was established in 1986 with the aim of protecting sea turtle eggs and raising hatchlings in a ]protected environment until they are strong enough to be released into the ocean. The hatchery is run by a non-profit organization and is funded through donations and entrance fees. Visitors can learn about the different species of sea turtles found in Sri Lanka, as well as the threats they face from habitat destruction, poaching, and pollution.

The hatchery also provides education and awareness programs for local communities and schools.At the hatchery, visitors can see different stages of the sea turtle life cycle, from eggs to hatchlings to adult turtles. They can also participate infeeding and caring for the turtles and even release them into the ocean when they are ready.The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery has contributed significantly to the conservation of sea turtles in SriLanka, and the project has been recognized by international organizations for its conservation efforts. It is a popular tourist attraction and a must-visit destination for those interested in marine conservation and wildlife

Functions Performed..

1. Collection and Incubation of Eggs: The hatchery collects eggs that female sea turtles have left on the shores and brings them inside for incubation. This helps to protect the eggs from risks like predators disturbance from people, and other possible hazards. The hatchery provides a suitable environment for the eggs to develop and the young turtles to live.ive.

2.Treatment and rehabilitation for sick and damaged turtles can be provided at the hatchery before they are released back into the wild. The hatchery staff takes care of these turtles and provides any essential medical attention.

3.Research and Monitoring: To maintain track of the turtles' whereabouts, behaviors, and population sizes, the hatchery crew monitors them. This helps in understanding the challenges encountered by turtles and in creating effective conservation plans.

4.Raising Awareness: To educate the public about the value of sea turtle protection, the hatchery offers engaging activities and experiences. The hatchery staff educates visitors on the threats faced by sea turtles and how they can help protect them.
